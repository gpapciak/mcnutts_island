Why This Matters

The Island's Layered History

McNutt's Island is not one story — it is many. Mi'kmaq presence for ten thousand years. Black Loyalist families who arrived in 1783 with promises of freedom and land. The lighthouse keepers who lived through Atlantic winters. The fishing families who still return each spring. The seasonal residents. The researchers. The people who crossed once in a kayak and never forgot the afternoon.

These stories are historical documents. They are also invitations to connection — to a place, to a community, and to an ongoing conversation about what this island has meant and what it might become.

We ask only that submissions be genuine. Stories, memories, and reflections — about the island in any era, from any perspective — are welcome. All submissions are moderated before appearing publicly.
