Who We Are

About the Alliance

The McNutt's Island Alliance is a community organization dedicated to the stewardship, celebration, and future of McNutt's Island, Nova Scotia.

Our Mission

What We're Here to Do

The McNutt's Island Alliance exists to connect the people who care about this island — residents and property owners, researchers and historians, the fishing community, visitors, and those who simply love this place from a distance — and to give that care a form.

We are not a government agency, a conservation land trust, or a developer. We are neighbours and advocates: people who believe that a place this historically significant and ecologically valuable deserves a community paying attention to it.

Our work falls into three broad areas:

  • Documentation — building the record of the island's history, ecology, and community
  • Stewardship — supporting the lighthouse preservation society, monitoring environmental health, promoting responsible access
  • Conversation — creating space for the difficult and important questions about the island's future, including the naming question
The Community

Who Belongs to the Alliance

Alliance membership is open to anyone with a connection to McNutt's Island — and we interpret "connection" broadly. Current and former island residents. Property owners. People whose families have fished the harbour for generations. Researchers. Lighthouse enthusiasts. South Shore Nova Scotians with roots in the community. Birders who've made the crossing once and never forgotten it.

There are no fees. No obligations beyond adding your name. The directory is simply a record that this community exists — and a starting point for the conversations and relationships that follow.

Land Acknowledgment

Mi'kma'ki

McNutt's Island and the surrounding waters lie within Mi'kma'ki, the ancestral and unceded territory of the Mi'kmaq people. The Mi'kmaq have lived in relationship with this land and these waters for at least ten thousand years.

We acknowledge this history and our responsibility as guests on this land. We are committed to engaging in genuine partnership with Mi'kmaq communities — including the Sipekne'katik First Nation — as we navigate questions of the island's future and, specifically, the question of its name.
